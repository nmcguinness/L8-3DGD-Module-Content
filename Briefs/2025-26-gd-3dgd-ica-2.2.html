Overview

This assessment evaluates your developed technical specialisation within Unreal Engine that directly supports your team’s Collaborative Project (CP).

This is the Stage 2 extension of your Stage 1 technical specialisation work. By this point, you are expected to move beyond initial research and early experimentation and show that your chosen Unreal area has developed into a clear, evidenced, and critically analysed technical contribution to the group game.

On completion of Stage 2, you should have:

  • Continued and extended the same Unreal-focused technical specialisation introduced in Stage 1.
  • Produced a single Mahara page documenting your area, its relevance to the game, prototypes, iterative development, and critical analysis.
  • Produced a final screencast demonstrating and explaining your completed technical work.
  • Shown that your contribution is technically credible, relevant to the CP, and meaningfully integrated into the project.

This deliverable checks that your specialisation is not only defined and relevant, but also substantially developed, evidenced, and critically understood.

Mahara Portfolio Structure (Mandatory)

All work contributing to your 3D Game Development (3DGD) continuous assessments must remain completely separate from your broader Collaborative Project entries.

For Stage 2, you must use the following structure:

  1. Single Page:
    • Page title: “3DGD within CP”

This page should represent the continuation and extension of your Stage 1 work.
It should allow both 3DGD and CP examiners to clearly identify your individual 3DGD technical contribution.
A weekly reflective journal is not required for 3DGD.

Mahara Page Requirements (Stage 2)

You must produce one Mahara page only, titled exactly:

3DGD within CP

The page must be no more than 1600 words.

Your Mahara page must include the following:

1. Unreal Area Being Examined

  • Clearly define the specific Unreal technical specialisation being developed.
  • This should be more focused and technically precise than in Stage 1.
  • Examples may include materials, lighting, Blueprints systems, AI, procedural tools, shaders, UI systems, cinematics, animation systems, optimisation, or technical environment workflows.

2. Relevance to the Game

  • Explain how this work supports your team’s Collaborative Project.
  • Clarify why this technical area matters to the game experience, production workflow, or final build quality.
  • Make your individual role and contribution easy to identify.

3. Technical Development

  • Present relevant development evidence such as:
    • screenshots
    • Blueprint snippets
    • Material graphs
    • system diagrams
    • workflow images
    • test scenes
    • implementation comparisons
  • Show how the work progressed from early tests into a more complete technical solution.

4. Evidence of Iterative Development

  • Demonstrate that the work developed over time through:
    • experimentation
    • testing
    • revision
    • refinement
    • response to problems or constraints
  • The page should make the process visible rather than only showing the final outcome.

5. Critical Analysis of the Completed Work

  • Evaluate the final result rather than simply describing it.
  • Discuss:
    • what worked well
    • what did not work as expected
    • what limitations remain
    • what compromises were made
    • what you learned
    • what you would improve if development continued

At this stage, the expectation is not just evidence of activity, but evidence of development, judgement, and technical understanding.

Screencast Requirements

You must submit a single screencast of no more than 8–10 minutes in duration. The screencast should clearly present your final technical work and explain its role within the collaborative project.

Screencast Content

Your private YouTube screencast should include:

  • A brief explanation of your technical specialisation.
  • A demonstration of the completed work in Unreal.
  • A clear explanation of how it supports the group game.
  • Brief reference to important prototypes, iterations, or revisions.
  • A short critical reflection on strengths, weaknesses, and learning.

Your screencast should be named using the following format:

2025-26-GD-3DGD-TS2-StudentName

The screencast is not just a silent video capture. It should function as a concise, guided technical walkthrough of your completed contribution.
